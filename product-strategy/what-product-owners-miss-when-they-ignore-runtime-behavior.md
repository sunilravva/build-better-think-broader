# What Product Owners Miss When They Ignore Runtime Behavior

> **Published:** LinkedIn Newsletter - Build Better, Think Broader  
> **Topic:** Product Strategy  
> **Read Time:** 1 min

## Overview

Most product decisions are made in planning rooms. Whiteboards, roadmaps, and user stories outline what products should do. But products don't live in planning rooms—they live in production, where runtime behavior dictates actual user experience.

## The Problem

Product Owners often prioritize features based on requirements and expected use cases. What gets missed? How the product behaves under real-world conditions:
- **API response times** when systems are under load
- **Data synchronization delays** between microservices  
- **Memory consumption** during peak usage
- **Error handling** when third-party services fail

These aren't just engineering concerns—they're product concerns.

## Why It Matters

A beautifully designed feature means nothing if:
- Users abandon it because it's too slow
- Data appears stale or inconsistent
- The system crashes during high traffic

## The Product Owner's Role

Product Owners must:
1. **Partner with engineering** to understand runtime constraints
2. **Define non-functional requirements** (latency, availability, scalability) as acceptance criteria
3. **Test in production-like environments** before declaring features "done"
4. **Monitor post-release behavior** to validate assumptions

## Key Takeaway

Great Product Owners don't just define what products should do. They ensure products actually do it—reliably, at scale, in production.

Runtime behavior isn't an engineering detail. It's a product differentiator.
