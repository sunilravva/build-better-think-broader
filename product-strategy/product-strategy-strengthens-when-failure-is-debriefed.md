# Product Strategy Strengthens When Failure Is Debriefed Properly

> **Published:** LinkedIn Newsletter - Build Better, Think Broader  
> **Topic:** Product Strategy  
> **Read Time:** 2 min

## Overview

Most teams say they want to learn from failure. In reality, many just want to move on quickly. But failure without debrief is wasted learning.

## The Challenge

When a feature underperforms, a project misses deadlines, or a market entry fails, the instinct is to:
- Find someone to blame
- Apply a quick fix
- Move to the next initiative

What gets lost? The systemic understanding of **why** it failed.

## Why Proper Debrief Matters

A great debrief answers:
1. **What was our assumption?** (Not what went wrong, but what did we believe would work?)
2. **What did we learn?** (What was different from our expectation?)
3. **What will we do differently?** (Not a blame assignment—a learning capture)
4. **How does this change strategy?** (The critical missing piece)

## The Product Owner's Role

Product Owners must:
- **Lead the debrief conversation**, not delegate it
- **Create psychological safety** so teams share honestly
- **Extract patterns** across multiple failures
- **Update the product strategy** based on learnings

## Key Takeaway

Failures are gifts wrapped in disappointment. The debrief is unwrapping them. Without it, you just have disappointment.
