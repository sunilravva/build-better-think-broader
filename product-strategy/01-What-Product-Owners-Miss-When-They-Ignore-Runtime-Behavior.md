## 🎯 Core Concept
Product decisions based only on requirements and metrics often fail because they ignore how the system actually behaves under real-world conditions. Understanding runtime behavior is understanding your actual customers.

---

## 📌 Key Takeaways

### 1. **The Gap Between Design and Reality**
- Requirements say one thing; production says another
- Metrics can be misleading without understanding the "why"
- Real user behavior often contradicts assumptions
- Runtime insights reveal hidden product opportunities

### 2. **What Runtime Behavior Tells You**
- Where users are struggling (not using features as designed)
- Unexpected usage patterns that reveal actual needs
- Performance bottlenecks that impact user experience
- Edge cases that break your perfect design

### 3. **Production Monitoring is Product Strategy**
- Logs and metrics are data about customer behavior
- Error patterns indicate real pain points
- User session flows reveal true product value
- System performance directly impacts perceived quality

### 4. **Ignoring Runtime Means Making Wrong Decisions**
- You'll optimize the wrong things
- You'll miss actual customer needs
- You'll build features no one uses
- You'll waste resources on non-problems

### 5. **Building Runtime Awareness**
- Invest in comprehensive observability
- Read production logs and error traces
- Study actual user sessions
- Have engineers share runtime insights with product

---

## 💡 Key Insights

> **Your Requirements Lie. Your Logs Tell the Truth.**  
Users don't follow the happy path. They find creative ways to break your assumptions. That's where real product insights live.

> **Runtime is Your Canary.**  
Performance degradation, errors, and unexpected usage patterns are early warnings that your product assumptions are wrong.

> **Observability is a Competitive Advantage.**  
Teams that understand their systems' runtime behavior make faster, smarter product decisions.
